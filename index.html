Social Protection Coverage:


Limited Coverage:

Only a small percentage of vulnerable populations in low-income countries received social protection cash benefits in 2020. This indicates a significant gap in coverage, leaving many individuals without adequate support during times of need.

Disaster Vulnerability:


Higher Risk:

Least developed countries, small island developing states, and landlocked developing countries face a higher risk of deaths and missing persons due to disasters. This vulnerability can be attributed to factors such as limited infrastructure, lack of early warning systems, and geographic exposure to natural hazards.

Government Spending:


Increased Investment:

Global governments have increased their spending on education, health, and social protection, demonstrating a commitment to addressing poverty and improving social well-being. However, the level of investment may still be insufficient to meet the needs of vulnerable populations.
